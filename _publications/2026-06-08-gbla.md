Generative recommenders often have an encoder-decoder architecture. The quadratic complexity of the encoder with respect to sequence length prohibits processing long user sequences, while scaling the sequence length is known to improve recommendations. We address this problem by adapting linear-time causal attention from LLMs to the bidirectional case. Our proposed Gated Bidirectional Linear Attention combines 1D convolutions and gating techniques with kernelized linear attention. On H100 GPUs, the GBLA layer becomes faster than self-attention from FlashAttention-v3 starting at length 4096, and the speedup grows with history length, reaching up to an 8.2x per-layer speedup at length 32768. A hybrid encoder that interleaves self-attention and GBLA in a 1:2 ratio largely preserves retrieval quality at lengths up to 8192 -- the maximum length studied.
